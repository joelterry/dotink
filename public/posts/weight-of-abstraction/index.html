What drives abstraction? The laws of physics of abstractions.
software is a mechanics of abstractions, so we should understand the physics of it.

Cost vs weight

Cost considers lots of things -- perf, maintainability, compile-time, specific to programming.
Weight just refers to (1) Ease of creation, disposability (2) gravitational field

Mathematical notation

Most abstractions are very lightweight, use-once

Programming languages

Abstractions can be very heavy, very light.

Lisp has very small cost of abstraction -> good, but leads to overabstraction
Java has very large cost -> bad, in the worst way

Abstraction weights in language design

When we design languages, we should take care that the weights of abstractions reflect how we want people to use them.
e.g. functions should be lightweight if we want lots of disposable use. Packages should be heavyweight if we don't want npm hell.
Fast, light abstractions are not always good.
